SOM (Bunshaft)1952

Lever House

Midtown East · Tower

First curtain-wall office tower on Park Avenue. 21 stories of blue-green tinted glass on a slim slab raised over a horizontal podium, leaving most of the lot as public ground plane. Established the postwar Manhattan office-tower vocabulary that Seagram refined four years later.

↗ 390 Park Avenue

Mies van der Rohe + Philip Johnson1958

Seagram Building

Midtown East · Tower

Bronze-clad steel and amber glass, set back 90 feet from Park Avenue on a granite plaza — a gesture that triggered NYC's 1961 zoning revision permitting public-space bonuses. The plaza's twin reflecting pools and the Four Seasons interior (Johnson) constitute the canonical postwar American office tower.

↗ 375 Park Avenue

Olmsted + Vaux1858

Central Park

Manhattan · Park · 843 acres

Greensward Plan winner, 1858. Sunken transverse roads, the Ramble, the Mall, the Lake. The founding text of American landscape architecture and the political argument that public space precedes the city around it.

↗ Central Park, NY

Olmsted + Vaux1867

Prospect Park

Brooklyn · Park · 526 acres

Olmsted considered Prospect his and Vaux's superior work — a single uninterrupted lot allowed the design they couldn't achieve in Manhattan's gridded site. The Long Meadow, the Ravine, the Lake. More topographically integrated than Central.

↗ Prospect Park, Brooklyn

James Corner FO + DS+R + Piet Oudolf2009 — 2014

The High Line

West Side · Linear Park · 1.45 miles

Adaptive reuse of the abandoned West Side Line freight viaduct. Three phases, designed as a hybrid of preserved rail bed, planted meadow, and pedestrian infrastructure. The single most influential urban-design project of the 21st century — generated direct adaptations in dozens of cities.

↗ Gansevoort St to W 34th St

Brooklyn Bridge Park

Brooklyn waterfront · Park · 85 acres

Six rebuilt piers stitched together by salvage-timber promenades, salt marsh, and the deliberate retention of industrial topography. Stormwater management and habitat restoration treated as primary design generators rather than secondary engineering.

↗ DUMBO to Atlantic Ave

Four Freedoms Park

Roosevelt Island · Memorial Park

Kahn's last design, completed 38 years posthumously. Triangular allée of linden trees converging on a granite room open to the East River. Mount Mora granite blocks set with 1-inch gaps. The most precise public space ever built in New York.

Washington Square Park

Greenwich Village · Park · 9.75 acres

Stanford White's Beaux-Arts marble arch, 1892, replaced an earlier wooden version commemorating Washington's inauguration centenary. Originally a potter's field — bodies were encountered during the 2021 renovation. The most thoroughly programmed public square in lower Manhattan.

↗ Washington Sq, Greenwich Village

Echo Park · Park · 29 acres

29-acre artificial lake — original 1892 reservoir converted to a park in 1895. The lotus bed (the largest outside Asia), the fountain, the swan boats. $45 million restoration completed 2013. The most photographed view of downtown's skyline from a foreground of palm trees and water.

Grand Central Market

Downtown LA · Market · $

30,000 sq ft, 40+ stalls. Sarita's Pupuseria, Sticky Rice (Thai), Lucky Bird (fried chicken), McConnell's (ice cream), Egg Slut (the original location). LA's most legible cross-section of the city's food culture under one ceiling. Open daily until 9pm.

Go Get Em Tiger

Highland Park + 8 locations · Coffee

Founded by US Barista Champions Kyle Glanville and Charles Babinski (formerly of Intelligentsia). Multiple locations across LA. Their almond-macadamia milk latte was named the best iced latte in America by the New York Times. House-made nut milks; rotating seasonal single-origins.
